Overview

Purpose

Our goal is to create a place where sterotypes and stigmas no longer exist. A community where the majority is seen rather than the extremes. Anime is an artistic style of storytelling that is beloved by many, yet there seem to be a large majority who have never even given it a chance. We intend to properly demonstrate the appeal in order to educate the ignorant and help bring peace to those who feel alone in their interests.

Audience

The target audience is both males and females from the age range of 14-32. We are looking to appeal to those who are old enough that they start to develop their social skills and young enough that they may not yet have the responsibility and a family. The target audience is in this range because we are looking for those who enjoy anime and are in a situation that they may want to introduce their friends to it as well. That demographic seems to be the most densely populated for those that both watch anime and choose to spend more of their time around friends rather than family.

Content

Home page

Japanese animation, more commonly referred to as “anime”, has been a popular genre of an artistic style of storytelling originating as far back as the early 1900's. Although Japanese animation has always had influence in the Americas, it has exploded in popularity since 2020, largely due to quarantining as a result of COVID 19. When the world entered a social distancing period and underwent a lockdown, many of us turned to technology and media in order to keep ourselves entertained, discovering new forms of entertainment. One such form of entertainment was anime. As you navigate this website, hopefully we are educate you on what exactly anime is and why you WILL want to watch it.

Anime vs Live Action

It is no secret that animation is typically associated with children shows and movies giving animation a bad connotation; however, there are some storytelling styles and thematic elements that can be hard to achieve in a live action production. For example, have you ever watched a movie with bad CGI or totally obvious green screening? Did that out-of-the-ordinary-look take away from the movie at all? Did it elicit some sort of negative response from you as the viewer? If so, then you experienced the downfall of live action television. We observe the world and record in our brain our observations every day. We, as people, internalize all that we see and when the effect of a movie is very apparent that it is fake, it takes us out of that story. Marvel is an excellent example of when we see some fantasy exist in an alternate reality to ours. Although we know that a man should not be able to turn into a green giant monster, because we observe that it interacts with an environment that is similar to ours, our brain can accept that as real in another universe. When not done well though, we can see it and have a hard time believing in that story. Anime does not have this downfall. Anime is not a world we live in and therefore there are very few things we can witness in an anime that would lead us to believe it is fake. Teeth turning into spikes, faces turning red from embarrassment, body and limbs changing shape. These are a few artistic elements that compliment the narrative of the story. These visuals help the viewer to gain a sense of how that character is feeling. There is little confusion and because there is no concern for being taken out of the plot, it is easier to become more emotionally invested.

Popular Anime

When talking about the most popular anime it is important to mention what is considered to be the father of anime, “Dragon Ball”. The Dragon Ball series is considered to be the father of anime because it was the first to have widespread popularity across the globe. You may have heard of Dragon Ball or Dragon Ball Z once already. It would be weird to not have heard of it at least once because for many it was their first introduction into anime. From there we have “The Big Three”. The Big Three is a group of three anime that dominated the industry for years where the manga sales and anime adaptations were top sellers for decades. The three are “Naruto”, “Bleach”, and “One Piece”. Once again, you may have heard of one of these three already. For many, these may have been someone's introduction into anime as well. Because of the timeline for the release and runtime of these anime you could make the argument that Dragon Ball was the first generation wave of anime influence and the Big Three to be the second. There has been a debate about whether or not there has been a resurgence of a third wave. A new generation of anime that has taken over pop culture. “My Hero Academia”, “JuJutsu Kaisen”, “Attack on Titan”, or “Demon Slayer” to name a few. With many of those titles appearing in theater releases and achieving a top 10 series ranking on IMDB, next to series like “Breaking Bad”, it is undeniable that these newer generation anime have been gaining more traction in the US than ever before.
